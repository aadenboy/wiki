Glass is an infrared-colored quadrupedal cag with a gelatinous body, a sister to Sauce, Granola, Bismuth, and Chalk. She has pointed legs similar to Linda's, two round tufts on each cheek, and a bushy tail.

Personality

She is a passive listener and astute observer, but also enthusiastically chaotic. She will often be playful and competitive, usually teasing and contorting her siblings as she feels like it. She enjoys using her body utility-wise much like Sauce, but it's more often for her own gains and antics. She doesn't necessarily lack empathy, but doesn't understand grief in the same way, and usually isn't ever annoyed or resentful towards anyone, often simply being confused instead.

Characteristics

Her body is defined as being an infrared color. Because this is outside of human and cag vision, she is simply portrayed as an indiscernibly dark red. Whether or not she only reflects or constantly emits infrared light is left ambiguous.

Her body is composed of an elastic material which can easily be deformed and shaped into any shape much like clay. She is unable to feel any pain, and is able to choose to "reset" her body at any point, instantly reverting all changes made back to normal. The inside of her body is consistent all the way through, with no organs; the only cavity being her mouth. In this way, she does not have any biological functions, and does not need to eat nor breathe. Her body can easily hold most forms given to her, but may also slouch or crumple due to a lack of support. If segmented, only the largest part nearest the perceived head region remains in control. Glass is also only able to move whatever limbs are defined in the way they normally move. Her body form and mental state are independent and do not control each other.

Between her siblings, she is the most malleable with a lack of springiness.

Her entire body, while smooth, is not wet or moist, more similar in material to a smooth clay. She can be melted and frozen, becoming more pliable or more rubbery on either extreme, at the loss of movement.

Her speech bubble is the simple label model, with the text and connector colored his body color with no infill (though it may be white for contrast). The typeface is somewhat sloppy and freeform—the same as her handwriting.

Style guide

When drawing Glass, portray a smooth form rather than an irregular one, as she isn't lumpy nor porous. Her head, cheek tufts, and tail are composed of spheres, while her body and legs are generally one shape, with the legs connected by smooth arcs. Her ears are cones. You should not imply any fur or texture, as her surface is smooth and featureless. If you are shading, her surface should be highly shiny and somewhat specular.
